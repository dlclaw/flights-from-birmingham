Birmingham to Dubai: Your Gateway to Arabia

Dubai is one of Birmingham Airport's most prestigious routes, offering direct connections to this ultra-modern desert metropolis. Whether you're visiting for luxury shopping, desert adventures, or connecting to Asia and Australia, this route provides excellent options.

Flight Information

Direct Flights

Emirates operates direct flights from Birmingham to Dubai, offering world-class service on modern aircraft. Direct flights provide the most convenient option, saving you time and hassle.

Flight Duration

  • Direct: Approximately 6 hours 45 minutes
  • With 1 stop: 8-12 hours depending on connection

When to Fly

Book 2-4 months in advance for the best prices. Consider:

  • Cheapest months: May-September (very hot but lower prices)
  • Peak season: November-March (perfect weather, higher prices)
  • Best value: October or April (good weather, moderate prices)

Visa Requirements

UK passport holders can obtain a free 30-day tourist visa on arrival at Dubai Airport. Ensure your passport is valid for at least 6 months from your arrival date.

What to Do in Dubai

Iconic Attractions

  • Burj Khalifa: World's tallest building - book observation deck tickets in advance
  • Dubai Mall: Massive shopping complex with aquarium and ice rink
  • Dubai Fountain: Spectacular water show (free, multiple times daily)
  • Palm Jumeirah: Man-made island with luxury resorts and beaches
  • Burj Al Arab: Iconic sail-shaped hotel (book afternoon tea to visit inside)
  • Dubai Marina: Modern waterfront with dining and entertainment

Cultural Experiences

  • Old Dubai & Gold Souk: Traditional markets and historic Al Fahidi district
  • Jumeirah Mosque: Beautiful mosque open to non-Muslim visitors
  • Dubai Museum: Learn about emirate's transformation
  • Abra Boat Ride: Traditional water taxi across Dubai Creek

Desert Adventures

  • Desert Safari: Dune bashing, camel rides, and BBQ dinner
  • Hot Air Balloon: Sunrise flight over the desert
  • Quad Biking: Adventure in the dunes
  • Overnight Camping: Sleep under the stars in Bedouin-style camps

Getting Around Dubai

  • Dubai Metro: Modern, air-conditioned, and efficient (doesn't serve all areas)
  • Taxis: Plentiful and relatively affordable
  • Uber/Careem: Ride-sharing apps widely used
  • Rental Car: Good for exploring beyond the city
  • Bus: Extensive network but can be complex for tourists

Best Areas to Stay

  • Downtown Dubai: Near Burj Khalifa, high-end hotels
  • Dubai Marina: Modern, beachside, great nightlife
  • Jumeirah Beach: Resort area, family-friendly
  • Deira/Bur Dubai: Traditional areas, budget-friendly
  • Palm Jumeirah: Luxury island resort

Important Travel Tips

  • Currency is UAE Dirham (AED) - roughly ¬£1 = 4.5 AED
  • Dubai is very hot May-September (40-45¬∞C) - stay hydrated
  • Dress modestly in public areas (shoulders and knees covered)
  • Friday is the main day off (like Sunday in UK)
  • Alcohol only available in licensed venues (hotels/restaurants)
  • Tipping 10-15% is customary in restaurants
  • Public displays of affection are frowned upon
  • Dubai is very safe but respect local customs and laws

Shopping in Dubai

  • Dubai Mall: One of the world's largest shopping centers
  • Mall of the Emirates: Home to Ski Dubai indoor ski slope
  • Gold Souk: Traditional market with competitive prices
  • Dubai Duty Free: Excellent airport shopping
  • Dubai Shopping Festival: January-February with massive discounts

Connecting Flights via Dubai

Dubai International Airport is a major global hub. You can connect to:

  • Southeast Asia (Bangkok, Singapore, Kuala Lumpur)
  • Australia and New Zealand
  • Indian Subcontinent (Mumbai, Delhi, Colombo)
  • Africa (Nairobi, Cape Town, Johannesburg)
  • East Asia (Hong Kong, Tokyo, Seoul)

Emirates offers excellent connections with competitive prices and high service standards.
